Blog Reflection Paper

Developing the Information Technology Blog illustrated the value of blogging best practices I researched early on: writing with a clear voice, structuring posts for skimmability, and weaving visual assets throughout to maintain reader engagement. I leaned on consistent typography, descriptive headings, and accessible alt text so each post felt polished and professional. Along the way I hit a few snags, including balancing media-heavy sections with performance and aligning the navigation across pages; I resolved them by optimizing image sizes, testing layouts on multiple devices, and iterating the header structure until everything remained responsive.

The finished blog now serves as an educational tool that can anchor discussions in future IT courses, offering classmates a living example of how theory, labs, and industry research translate into public-facing artifacts. I intend to keep the site current by revisiting each post after major coursework milestones, layering in new insights, and expanding the reflection page with evidence of growth—analytics snapshots, updated resources, and summaries of emerging technologies. A quarterly content review schedule will help me maintain accuracy, incorporate peer feedback, and continue sharpening my storytelling voice throughout my academic journey.
